Build Your Own Particle Physics Detector

Instructions:

To build your detector, drag detector layers (big "x") into the circles.
Each layer is responsible for detecting different types of particles.
Press SPACE or "Fire Particle" to simulate a particle collision!
Press C or "Clear Tracks" to clear all tracks.

Game Rules:

  • Goal: Build a detector that can identify different particle types!

Glossary:

  • ECAL (Electromagnetic Calorimeter): Detects and measures energy of photons and electrons by absorbing them.
  • HCAL (Hadronic Calorimeter): Detects and measures energy of hadrons (like protons and neutrons) by absorbing them.
  • Magnet: Creates a magnetic field that causes charged particles to curve, helping to determine their charge (and momentum).
  • Muon chambers: Detect muon tracks, which can penetrate through other layers.
  • Tracker: Measures the trajectory of charged particles as they pass through different layers.

Particle types:

  • Photons - Are the lightest particles and get absorbed by ECAL, HCAL, Magnet. They have no charge, so they don't curve in the magnetic field.
  • Electrons - Just as photons, they get absorbed by ECAL, HCAL, Magnet, and Muon chambers. However, they are charged and will curve in the magnetic field.
  • Hadrons - Heavier particles, and get absorbed by HCAL, Magnet, Muon chambers.
  • Muons - Pass through all the layers, but leave signal in the Muon chambers.
